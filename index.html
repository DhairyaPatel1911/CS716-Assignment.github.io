Program Overview

Welcome to the MSc in Data Science program at the University of Regina! Our program provides a solid academic foundation in statistics, machine learning, AI, and big data analytics. Whether you're pursuing a career in industry or academia, our program equips you with the skills needed to analyze big data and apply innovative solutions to real-world problems.

Curriculum

The MSc in Data Science curriculum is designed to combine theoretical knowledge with practical applications, covering a broad range of topics from foundational data science concepts to advanced AI and big data techniques. We've integrated courses from top universities to provide students with a well-rounded and modern education in the field.

Core Courses

  • Statistical Modeling for Data Science
  • Big Data Analytics
  • Machine Learning
  • Data Mining and Predictive Modeling
  • Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization
  • Probability and Statistics for Data Science

Elective Tracks

Choose two of the following:

  • Advanced Artificial Intelligence
  • Health Informatics
  • Big Data and Large-Scale Computing
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Ethics and Governance in Data Science

Why Choose Us?

Our MSc in Data Science offers a unique blend of interdisciplinary learning, research opportunities, and industry connections.

  • Interdisciplinary Learning: Learn from experts in computer science, statistics, and applied mathematics.
  • State-of-the-Art Facilities: Access cutting-edge technology and computational tools.
  • Industry Connections: Internships and capstone projects with top industry partners, ensuring valuable real-world experience.
  • Faculty Expertise: Collaborate with renowned faculty working on cutting-edge research in AI, big data, and health informatics.

Admissions

We are looking for candidates with a strong academic background in computer science, statistics, or related fields. Applicants should have experience in programming, data analysis, and a strong foundation in mathematics.

Application Deadlines

  • Fall Admission: June 1
  • Winter Admission: October 1
